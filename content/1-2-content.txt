======================================================
speculation
[,spekju'leiʃən]
写(核心)
n.
沉思，思索；设想，推测;投机，投机事业
noun
1.
【释义】
when you guess about the possible causes or effects of something without knowing all the facts, or the guesses that you make
猜测，推测:
【例句】
There is speculation that the president is ill.
有人猜测总统病了。
【例句】
speculation about the future
对未来的推测
【例句】
The witness's statement was pure speculation (=not based on any facts) .
这名证人的陈述纯粹是猜测。
2.
【释义】
when you try to make a large profit by buying goods, property, SHARES etc and then selling them
投机买卖，投机生意:
【例句】
property speculation
房地产投机买卖

ritual
['ritjuəl; -tʃu-]
听
写(核心)
n.
典礼,宗教仪式,例行公事,常规
adj.
典礼的,宗教仪式的
noun
1.
【释义】
a ceremony that is always performed in the same way, in order to mark an important religious or social occasion
〔为纪念重要宗教或社会事件举行的〕仪式，典礼;
【例句】
ancient pagan rituals
古老的异教仪式
【例句】
the importance of religion and ritual in our lives
我们生活中宗教和宗教仪式的重要性
【例句】
The lady of the house performs the sacred ritual of lighting two candles.
由女主人来完成点燃两根蜡烛这一神圣的仪式。
2.
【释义】
something that you do regularly and in the same way each time
惯例，老规矩，例行公事;
【例句】
the daily ritual of mealtimes
每天固定不变的进餐时间
【例句】
He went through the ritual of lighting his cigar.
他按照老习惯点燃雪茄。
adjective
1.
【释义】
done as part of a rite or ritual
作为仪式一部分的:
【例句】
ritual dances
仪式舞蹈
2.
【释义】
done in a fixed and expected way, but without real meaning or sincerity
例行的:
【例句】
The police issued the usual ritual apology.
警方发表了一贯例行公事式的道歉。
【例句】
Animals are brought in and ritually slaughtered.
动物被抬进来祭宰。
